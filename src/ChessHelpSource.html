Commands

Moves:

  • Enter moves in the format "c# c#" where c is
    a character (a-h) and # is a number (1-8).
  • The legacy move command of the form
    "move (#,#) -> (#,#)" is still supported.

Undo:

  • Undo moves by clicking "Undo" or typing "undo".

Log:

  • Show the log by clicking "Show log" or typing
    "log".

Quit:

  • Quit by closing the game window or typing
    "please quit".

Change Names:

  • Change the player names by clicking
    "Change names" or typing "names".

Help:

  • Access this screen by clicking "Help" or typing
    "help".

Piece rules

Pawn:

  • Can move one or two squares forward
    on the first move.
  • Can move only one square forward
    after the first move.
  • Can capture diagonally, 1 square away.
  • Can become a Queen, Rook, Bishop or Knight
    if it reaches the other end of board.

Rook:

  • Can move along a rank or file, by any number
    of empty squares.

Bishop:

  • Can move diagonally any number of
    empty squares.
  • Always stays on the same color squares.

Knight:

  • Moves in a capital "L" shape.
  • Can jump over any pieces in its way.
  • Can only capture pieces at its final destination.

Queen:

  • Can move in any direction any number of
    empty spaces.
  • Is the most powerful piece on the board.

King:

  • Can move one space in any direction.
  • The game is over when the king is trapped in
    checkmate.

Other

Castling:

  • To castle, both the king and the rook
    may not have moved.
  • If the spaces between them are clear,
    attempt to move the king to its new position
    (two squares to its left or right).
  • If successful, both the king and the rook
    will move to their new spaces.

En Passant

  • A pawn can take an opposing pawn that moves
    two squares on its first move if it would have
    been able to take it had it only moved one square.

Who moves first?

  • The player with white chess pieces always
    moves first.
